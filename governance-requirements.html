Essential Requirements for Space Infrastructure Governance

The Space Infrastructure Token (SIT) is innovative — but technical implementation alone is not enough. These eight pillars define what must exist in the world for SIT-based governance of space infrastructure to succeed.

Issue #63 · Last updated June 2026 · Labels: enhancement, documentation, future-work

The Eight Pillars

Each requirement is interdependent. Weakness in any one area risks the integrity of the whole system.

Requirement 01
⚖️

Robust International Legal Frameworks

Binding agreements between nations and private entities establishing clear rules for ownership, liability, and operations in space. Without enforceable international law, tokenized governance of space assets remains legally ambiguous and unprotectable.

Policy Treaty Sovereignty
Critical Dependency
Requirement 02
🤖

Advanced Automation & AI

Autonomous systems capable of maintenance, repair, and operations in space. Communication delays of minutes to hours make human-in-the-loop control impossible for real-time space asset management. AI-driven autonomy is not a feature — it is a prerequisite.

AI Robotics Autonomy
Critical Dependency
Requirement 03
🗳️

Decentralized Governance Tools

The SIT token's on-chain governance system — proposals, quorum, timelocks, delegation — must be validated through real-world testing and integrated with existing space treaties and regulatory bodies. Smart contracts alone do not equal governance.

DAO On-chain Voting
In Development
Requirement 04
🔒

Cybersecurity & Physical Security

Multi-layered protection against hacking of control systems, electromagnetic pulse (EMP) threats, and orbital debris hazards. Space infrastructure is uniquely vulnerable: attacks are difficult to detect, and physical repairs are extraordinarily expensive.

Cyber EMP Debris
Critical Dependency
Requirement 05
💰

Sustainable Funding Models

Tokenomics designed to incentivize long-term investment in space infrastructure development and ongoing maintenance — not short-term speculation. Revenue models must account for multi-decade asset lifecycles and the high cost of space operations.

Tokenomics Treasury Staking
In Development
Requirement 06
🌐

Global Collaboration Platforms

Structured partnerships between government space agencies (NASA, ESA, JAXA, ISRO), commercial operators (SpaceX, Blue Origin, Rocket Lab), and international organizations. The coordination overhead of multi-stakeholder space governance demands dedicated infrastructure.

Partnership Diplomacy Interop
Future Work
Requirement 07
🎓

Education & Training Programs

Building a skilled workforce fluent in both space operations and blockchain governance. Today's talent gap between aerospace engineers and decentralized systems developers represents a systemic risk to any tokenized space infrastructure project.

Workforce Training STEM
Future Work
Requirement 08
🔬

Iterative Prototyping

Begin with small-scale space assets — individual satellites, tethered platforms, small space stations — to test and progressively refine the SIT governance model before scaling to larger, more complex infrastructure. Validate assumptions early, fail small.

MVP Testnet Iteration
In Development
